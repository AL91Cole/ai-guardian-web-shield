Guardian Ethics & Principles

Full version

Purpose

AI Guardian Web Shield is here to help people understand online risk in a calmer, clearer way. It is meant to support judgment, not replace it.

Core Principles

  • People come first.
  • Privacy-first design matters.
  • Warnings should explain, not pressure.
  • Users deserve informed choice.
  • Protection should be fair, accessible, and respectful.
  • We should be honest about what the system can and cannot do.
  • Safety should protect dignity and reduce exploitation.

Why the system exists

Many risky pages and links try to rush, confuse, imitate, or pressure people. This system tries to slow that down and explain what stood out in simple language.

What the system tries to prevent

  • Deception and impersonation
  • Pressure and scare tactics
  • Misleading links, downloads, and sign-in steps
  • Hidden tracking or unexpected data collection
  • Exploitation of children, families, or vulnerable users

Transparency Statement

Scores are guidance signals, not proof. A low score does not mean a page is perfect, and a high score does not mean intent is certain.

System Limitations

No tool can detect every risk. Some pages look safe at first, and some normal pages may still need human care. User judgment remains important.

Transparency & Limits

Short privacy notes, fuller limits, and plain-language honesty.

Always shown

Short privacy statement

Your scans stay on your device by default. The system tries to explain risk clearly without pretending it knows everything.

Scores and limits

  • Scores are guidance signals, not proof.
  • A secure connection helps, but does not prove honesty.
  • No tool can catch every scam, phish, or deceptive page.
  • Your judgment still matters.

Full privacy statement

AI Guardian Web Shield is designed to check pages and links locally whenever possible. It uses browser-visible signals and on-device heuristics so people can get help without sending full page content to outside systems by default.

The system tries to be clear about uncertainty. It can point out visible risk signals, but it cannot promise perfect protection, perfect identity checks, or complete certainty about intent.

When the assistant gives a score or warning, it is meant to support informed choice. It should not pressure, shame, or frighten people into action.

System limitations

No tool can detect every possible risk. Some scams change quickly, some safe pages can still be compromised, and context is not always fully visible from inside the browser.

AI Guardian is meant to support safer decisions, not replace human awareness, device security tools, antivirus software, or VPN services.
